Smart Farming.

In the last decades, the population has been quickly growing. In 2050, According to the United Nation, the world’s population is expected from 9.7 billion. This overpopulation is creating one question: how are we going to supply enough food? Around the world, it is debated about agriculture and stockbreeding’s old techniques. For this reason, the technology is offered as a great solution with smart farming.

Some years ago, it was impossible to obtain big data because of high prices and the machines were not enough ability. But now, the technology has been evolving and it has created smart farming. This area joins fields such as robotic, computer-based imaging, PS technology, climate forecasting, environmental controls, sensing technologies, software apps, telematics, and hardware. All of them analyze, inspect, and forecast crops and livestock where it is obtained for better results and reducing cost.
